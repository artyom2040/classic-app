A

Adagio

[a-da-jio]

Slow and stately (literally, "at ease"). Often used as the second movement in a symphony.

chevron_right

Allegro

[al-leh-gro]

Fast, quick, and bright.

chevron_right

Andante

[an-dan-te]

Moderately slow, at a walking pace.

chevron_right

B

Baroque

[buh-roke]

A style of European architecture, music, and art of the 17th and 18th centuries.

chevron_right

C

Cadence

[kay-dence]

A sequence of chords that brings an end to a phrase, either in the middle or the end of a composition.

chevron_right

Crescendo

[kruh-shen-doh]

A gradual increase in loudness.

chevron_right

P

Pizzicato

[pit-si-kah-toh]

Playing a string instrument by plucking the strings rather than using the bow.

chevron_right

S

Sonata Form

[so-na-ta form]

A musical structure consisting of exposition, development, and recapitulation.
